Polish-Ukrainian Workshop

 ‘Mathematical  Models  of  Transport Processes

 in  Health and Disease’

Kyiv, Ukraine.                                                                             June 13, 2019.

 

Everybody who deals with  mathematical modeling  Transport Processes   arising in  healthy and broken/deformed tissue, bones, kidney, tumour etc. is welcome to participate. Each prospective participant should send the author name(s), affiliation, the title of the talk proposed, and the abstract to the address:  iammp@ukr.net

Organizers:

Professor Roman Cherniha (Kyiv)

Dr. Vasyl’ Davydovych (Kyiv)

Dr. Jan Poleszczuk (Warsaw)

Professor Jacek Waniewski (Warsaw)

The workshop organizers plan to select approximately 10 oral presentations (20 or 30 min. for each one). Participation without a talk is also possible – please send the registration details (name and surname, affiliation and contact address) to the same email address.

The organizers are going to establish the Workshop  as a regular meeting, which will take place  on a  periodical basis in Kyiv and Warsaw  in order to make stronger collaboration between the scholars of both countries involved in mathematical modeling with biomedical applications. Negotiations with a well-known international publisher  concerning publication of workshop proceedings   are  also foreseen.

There is no fee for participation in this event.

The workshop program will be available  on  June 7.

Deadline for the talk proposals:  June 2.

Deadline for the registration details without  talks: June 8.

The list of the participants and the title talks

(Last time updated:    May 27, 2019)

  1. R. Cherniha (Institute of Mathematics, NASU, Kyiv)  TBA
  2. T. Mel’nyk (Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv): Asymptotic analysis of a mathematical model of the  atherosclerosis development.
  3. J. Poleszczuk  (Institute of Biocybernetics and Biomedical Engineering, PAS, Warsaw): Pulse wave propagation modeling: how characteristics of blood flow can be used to assess the state of cardiovascular system.
  4. J. Stachowska-Pietka (Institute of Biocybernetics and Biomedical Engineering, PAS, Warsaw): Mathematical modelling of fluid and solute transport though the muscle tissue – a spatially distributed approach.
  5. Z. Szymańska (University of Warsaw and PAS): Mathematical modelling of intracellular protein dynamics: the importance of the spatial organisation of an eukaryotic cell.
  6.  J. Waniewski (Institute of Biocybernetics and Biomedical Engineering, PAS, Warsaw): New investigations in the theory of hemodialyzer.
  7. U. Lushchyk (Istyna Research Centre, Kyiv)  and  V. Novytskyy (Institute of Mathematics, NASU, Kyiv): Some aspects of clinical and instrumental objectivization dates modelling of tissue hydrophilicity processes in hemodynamic disorders.

Workshop  ‘Nonlinear Models in Mathematical Physics and  Biomathematics: Symmetry, Integrability,  Exact and Numerical Solutions’

Kyiv, Ukraine.                                                          December 21. 2018.

Everybody is welcome to participate who are solving the nonlinear mathematical models arising in nature. Each prospective participant should send the author name(s), the title of the talk proposed, and the abstract to the address:  iammp@ukr.net

The author(s) of the best presentation will be invited to publish (after peer-review) a paper with open access (but  without publication fee!) in the international journal Symmetry (2017 impact-factor 1.256)

https://www.mdpi.com/journal/symmetry

There is no fee for participation in this event.

Deadline for the talk proposals: December 10.

The workshop program will be available  on December 15.
